# UPI Payment Fraud – A Case Study

## What is it?
UPI fraud is when someone tricks you into sending them money 
through your UPI app by pretending to be someone trustworthy 
or by confusing you about how UPI works.

## How it happened – A Real Scenario

My cousin's friend Aryan was selling his old laptop on OLX 
last year. A guy called him saying he was an Army officer and 
wanted to buy it urgently. He seemed very professional on the 
phone so Aryan trusted him.

The guy said he had already sent the payment but accidentally 
sent extra money. He asked Aryan to "return" the extra amount. 
He then sent a UPI collect request to Aryan's phone and told 
him to just enter his PIN to confirm the refund.

Aryan entered his PIN thinking he was sending money back. 
But actually he had just approved a payment OUT of his own 
account. The so-called Army officer disappeared and Aryan 
lost Rs. 12,000 in under two minutes.

## Who gets targeted?
Mostly students and young people selling things online for 
the first time. Also elderly people and anyone who doesn't 
fully understand how UPI collect requests work.

## What are the consequences?
- Immediate financial loss with almost no way to recover it
- UPI transactions cannot be reversed once approved
- Mental stress and loss of trust in digital payments
- Some victims get targeted again by the same scammers

## The one thing to always remember
A UPI collect request ALWAYS takes money FROM you. 
There is no such thing as "receiving money by entering your PIN."
If someone says that — it is 100% a scam.

Source: Based on patterns reported on cybercrime.gov.in 
and personal accounts shared online (2024)
